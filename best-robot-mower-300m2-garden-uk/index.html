300m² is the classic British family lawn — big enough that push-mowing eats your Saturday, small enough that you don't need flagship hardware. The buying rule at this size: pick a mower rated ~400m² or above, so it never runs at its limit.

Quick answer

Best for 300m²: Segway Navimow i105E (£699, rated 500m²). Wired budget: Gardena Sileno Minimo 500 (~£429). Want AWD headroom: Navimow i208 AWD (£899, 800m²).

Why the i105E is the sweet spot

Its 500m² rating gives a 300m² lawn ~40% headroom: shorter daily sessions, less wear, and slack for awkward shapes, two zones, or that strip down the side of the house. Wire-free setup means the whole job is an app walk-round, and at ~£699 (often discounted) it's the cheapest mower we'd call a long-term buy at this size.

The headroom rule, explained once

Coverage ratings assume near-ideal conditions and generous daily mowing windows. Run a mower at 100% of its rating and it mows constantly, ages faster, and loses ground every rainy week. At 300m², a 400–600m² rating is the efficient band — beyond that you're paying for capacity you can't use.

What to avoid at 300m²

  • The Flymo GO 250 — great mower, wrong size; it's rated below your lawn.
  • Large-garden models (i215/i220 LiDAR) — you'd be paying £400+ for unused capacity, unless trees or size push you there.
  • Rating-equals-lawn-size buys — the whole trap this page exists to prevent.

FAQs

Is 300m² a small or medium lawn for a robot mower?

Medium-small — comfortably inside the range of affordable models. You don't need large-garden machines, but avoid mowers rated exactly 300m²: buy ~25% headroom so it isn't mowing flat-out daily.

How long does a robot mower take to cut 300m²?

It depends on the model's cutting rate and pattern, but the practical answer is: it doesn't matter. Robot mowers cut little and often on a schedule — the lawn is simply always done.

Can I get a decent robot mower for 300m² under £500?

Yes, wired: the Gardena Sileno Minimo 500 (~£429) covers 500m². Wire-free under £500 gets you the LawnMaster OcuMow, which is rated for lawns smaller than yours (150m² single-battery, 300m² dual) — stretch to the £699 Navimow i105E if you can.
