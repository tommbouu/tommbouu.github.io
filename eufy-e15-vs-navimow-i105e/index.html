Two of the best small-garden wire-free mowers in the UK, at wildly different prices: the Segway Navimow i105E at ~£699 and the Eufy E15 at £1,499. The price gap is the story — here's what doubling your spend actually buys, and when it's worth it.

Quick answer

Most small UK gardens: Navimow i105E — same-class coverage for half the money. Choose the E15 if: you want the absolute simplest setup (no antenna, ~15 minutes), your lawn is flat and even, and budget isn't the constraint.

The case for the Eufy E15

Reviewers (T3, TechRadar) rate it the mower for technophobes: no RTK antenna to site, near-silent running, excellent obstacle avoidance — great with pets and garden clutter — and a covered base station that keeps the mower dry. If your garden is flat, even, and you want zero fuss, it delivers.

The case for the Navimow i105E

It does the fundamental job — a reliably mowed lawn on a schedule, wire-free — for ~£699, with the biggest user base in the class. Its RTK setup is an extra 20 minutes once, not a recurring cost. And it's less picky about ground condition: the E15's documented damp-grass grip and uneven-terrain complaints don't apply.

Verdict

For most small UK gardens the i105E wins on value — the £800 saved buys a lot of blades and years of electricity. The E15 earns its price only in the specific case of a flat, even lawn owned by someone who wants the simplest possible setup, or heavy tree cover where RTK struggles.

FAQs

Why is the Eufy E15 so much more expensive than the Navimow i105E?

You're paying for camera-only navigation with no RTK antenna to install (~15-minute setup), a covered base station and Eufy's obstacle-avoidance stack. Whether that's worth roughly double the i105E's ~£699 depends on how much you value setup simplicity.

Which is better for a lawn with trees — E15 or i105E?

The E15: its cameras don't rely on satellite signal, while the i105E's RTK positioning degrades under heavy canopy. (The Navimow i208E with LiDAR is the third option here.)

Which handles rough or damp lawns better?

The i105E. Professional reviews consistently flag the E15's wheel grip on damp grass and its struggles on lightly uneven terrain — it's a flat-lawn specialist.
