Large AND steep?

The Luba 2 AWD's confirmed 80% (38.6°) slope rating makes it the default for big sloped ground. Mammotion's newer Luba 3 AWD (£2,099, 1,500–5,000m²) adds 360° LiDAR for tree-lined estates. At the premium end, the wired Husqvarna 535 AWD (£4,899, 3,500m²) buys the UK's strongest dealer network — worth it where downtime is unacceptable and someone else handles maintenance.

What to check before buying for a large garden

  • Zones and routes: can the mower physically travel between your lawns? Gates, steps and gravel paths decide this — check passage-width specs against your narrowest gap.
  • Per-charge coverage vs total rating: a 10,000m² rating means multiple sessions; check the per-day figure (Luba 2: up to 5,000m²/day).
  • Sky view across the whole plot: large gardens usually have trees — that's exactly where LiDAR earns its premium over pure RTK.
  • Terrain honesty: big rural lawns are rarely bowling greens. Reviewers flag camera-only flat-lawn machines (like the Eufy E15) struggling on damp, uneven ground — this category wants AWD or at least robust 2WD.

Common mistakes

  • Running at 100% of rating — a 1,000m² lawn needs a 1,300m²+ rating for weather slack.
  • Buying two small mowers instead of one right one — multi-zone mapping made this obsolete.
  • Paying flagship money for flat, open ground — the i215 at £1,099 covers a huge share of "large garden" cases; save the Luba premium for slopes and paddocks.

FAQs

How big a lawn can a robot mower handle in the UK?

Mainstream models top out around 10,000m² — the Mammotion Luba 2 AWD is rated 3,000–10,000m² and mows up to 5,000m² per day. Beyond that you're into professional machines. Most 'large' UK gardens (600–1,500m²) are comfortably covered by mid-range models like the Navimow i215 LiDAR.

Do I need multiple robot mowers for a big garden?

Usually not — modern multi-zone mapping lets one mower handle front and back lawns or separate paddocks, provided it can travel between them or you carry it. One correctly-rated mower beats two small ones.

Is Husqvarna worth the premium for large gardens?

Husqvarna's dealer network, servicing and parts availability are the strongest in the UK — that's what the premium buys. If you want the lowest price per m², the wire-free Mammotion and Navimow ranges undercut it by thousands.
