Best for most large gardens: Navimow i208E

The i208E pairs RTK with LiDAR, which matters on big plots because large gardens usually have trees — the very thing that degrades pure-RTK positioning. At £1,099 RRP it's the least expensive route to reliable large-lawn coverage in the wire-free world.

The dealer-backed option: Husqvarna 535 AWD

£4,899 buys the strongest UK dealer/service network in robot mowing and proven AWD on extreme slopes — with a boundary-wire install. For estates where downtime is unacceptable and someone else handles maintenance, it earns its price; for everyone else the wire-free options above cost a third as much.

What to avoid

  • Running at 100% capacity — a 1,000m² lawn needs a mower rated well above 1,000m².
  • Ignoring travel paths — check the mower can actually reach all zones (gates, steps, gravel).
  • Flat-lawn cameras on rough ground — reviewers flag the Eufy E15's grip on damp/uneven grass; big rural lawns are rarely bowling greens.

FAQs

How big a lawn can a robot mower handle in the UK?

Mainstream models top out around 5,000m² (e.g. Mammotion Luba 2 AWD 5000). Beyond that you're into professional/estate machines. Most 'large' UK gardens (600–1,500m²) are comfortably covered by mid-range models.

Do I need multiple robot mowers for a big garden?

Usually not — modern multi-zone mapping lets one mower handle front and back lawns or separate paddocks, provided it can travel between them or you carry it. One correctly-rated mower beats two small ones.

Is Husqvarna worth the premium for large gardens?

Husqvarna's dealer network, servicing and parts availability are the strongest in the UK — that's what the premium buys. If you want the lowest price per m², the wire-free Mammotion and Navimow ranges undercut it significantly.
