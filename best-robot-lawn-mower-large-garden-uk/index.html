What to check before buying for a large garden

  • Zones and routes: can the mower physically travel between your lawns? Gates, steps and gravel paths decide this — check passage-width specs against your narrowest gap.
  • Per-charge coverage vs total rating: a large rating means multiple sessions; check the per-day figure where the manufacturer publishes one.
  • Sky view across the whole plot: large gardens usually have trees — that's exactly where LiDAR earns its premium over pure RTK.
  • Terrain honesty: big rural lawns are rarely bowling greens. Reviewers flag camera-only flat-lawn machines (like the Eufy E15) struggling on damp, uneven ground — this category wants AWD or at least robust 2WD.

Common mistakes

  • Running at 100% of rating — a 1,000m² lawn needs a 1,300m²+ rating for weather slack.
  • Buying two small mowers instead of one right one — multi-zone mapping made this obsolete.
  • Paying flagship money for flat, open ground — the i215 at £1,099 covers a huge share of "large garden" cases; save the Husqvarna premium for genuinely oversized or steep plots.

FAQs

How big a lawn can a robot mower handle in the UK?

Among the wire-free brands AutoLawn UK currently has affiliate relationships with, the Navimow i220 LiDAR tops out at 2,000m². Beyond that, wired Husqvarna Automowers reach up to 3,500m² (535 AWD). Most 'large' UK gardens (600–1,500m²) are comfortably covered by mid-range models like the Navimow i215 LiDAR.

Do I need multiple robot mowers for a big garden?

Usually not — modern multi-zone mapping lets one mower handle front and back lawns or separate paddocks, provided it can travel between them or you carry it. One correctly-rated mower beats two small ones.

Is Husqvarna worth the premium for large gardens?

Husqvarna's dealer network, servicing and parts availability are the strongest in the UK — that's what the premium buys, along with coverage beyond what current wire-free options reach. If your lawn is under 2,000m², the Navimow range is far cheaper.
