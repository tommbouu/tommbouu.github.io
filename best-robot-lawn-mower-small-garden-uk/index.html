Quick answer

Best overall: Navimow i105E

Yes, it's rated to 500m² and your lawn might be 120m² — that headroom is a feature, not waste. The mower finishes fast, wears slowly, and copes with awkward shapes. Wire-free setup matters even more in small gardens, where a wire install wastes a disproportionate amount of the lawn's perimeter on excluded edges.

Budget pick: LawnMaster OcuMow 16

At ~£292 it's the cheapest wire-free mower we'd recommend in the UK. Drop it on the lawn, let the camera find the grass. Limits: simple lawns only, minimal scheduling, and coverage/battery specs we're still verifying. For a courtyard or terrace-house lawn, it's exactly enough.

The wired alternatives

The Flymo EasiLife GO 250 and Gardena Sileno Minimo are mature, quiet, reliable machines from the Husqvarna Group — if you don't mind pinning ~80–120m of wire round the edge one Saturday. In a small garden that's genuinely a one-afternoon job, which is why wired still makes sense at this size.

What small-garden buyers should avoid

  • Paying for coverage: anything rated 800m²+ is wasted money under 400m².
  • AWD and LiDAR: slope tech matters only if you have a slope; LiDAR matters only under heavy trees.
  • No-name sub-£250 imports: the OcuMow is the credible floor; below it, reliability and spares disappear.

FAQs

Is a robot mower worth it for a small garden?

Yes, arguably more than for big ones — small-garden models start around £292, and the time saved is the same whether your lawn takes 20 minutes or two hours to mow by hand. See our 'are they worth it' guide for the full maths.

What's the smallest lawn a robot mower makes sense for?

There's no real minimum — courtyard lawns of 30–50m² are fine for drop-and-mow models like the LawnMaster OcuMow. Below that, a cordless push mower is probably cheaper and simpler.

Do small gardens need RTK GPS navigation?

No. RTK's precision pays off on big open lawns. In a small garden, camera-based models work well and cost less — though RTK models like the Navimow i105E are still excellent if you want app scheduling and room to grow.
