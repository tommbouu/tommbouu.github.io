What cheap actually costs you

  • Coverage: budget models suit small lawns. Put a 250m² mower on a 400m² lawn and it will never finish.
  • Navigation: budget wired mowers bounce randomly rather than mowing lanes — fine for the lawn, slower overall.
  • Weather smarts: rain sensors and adaptive scheduling thin out at this price.

The false economies

  • No-name imports under £250: no UK spares, no support, resale value of zero.
  • Used mowers without the base station/wire kit: replacements often cost more than the saving.
  • Buying under-capacity: the £549 i108E on promo beats a £429 mower that's too small for your lawn — the wrong cheap mower is the most expensive option.

When to buy

Spring promos (March–May), Prime Day (July) and Black Friday (November) are the real windows — Navimow's spring 2026 sale cut up to £200. Our deals page tracks genuine drops.

FAQs

What is the cheapest robot lawn mower in the UK?

The cheapest we'd actually recommend is the LawnMaster OcuMow 16 at around £292 (checked July 2026). Cheaper no-name imports exist but lack UK support, spares and credible safety testing.

Are budget robot mowers reliable?

The branded budget tier (LawnMaster, Flymo, Gardena) is reliable within its limits: small, simple lawns. What you give up versus £700+ models is coverage, navigation sophistication and scheduling features — not basic dependability.

When do robot mowers go on sale in the UK?

Spring launch promotions (March–May), Prime Day (July), and Black Friday (November) are the consistent windows. Navimow ran up to £200 off in spring 2026, for example.
