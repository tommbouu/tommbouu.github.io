The false economies

  • No-name imports under £250: no UK spares, no support, resale value of zero.
  • Used mowers without the dock/wire kit: replacements often cost more than the saving.
  • Buying under-capacity: a £292 OcuMow on a 300m² lawn will never finish — the wrong cheap mower is the most expensive option. (The dual-battery OcuMow kit covers 300m² if you're set on it.)
  • Old "value pick" advice: several guides still cite the Navimow i108E as a budget option — it's currently £1,299 on the official UK store. Ignore stale pricing, including ours if the date at the top of this page looks old.

FAQs

What is the cheapest robot lawn mower in the UK?

The cheapest we'd actually recommend is the LawnMaster OcuMow 16 at around £292. Cheaper no-name imports exist but lack UK support, spares and credible safety testing.

Are budget robot mowers reliable?

The branded budget tier (LawnMaster, Flymo, Gardena) is reliable within its limits: small, simple lawns. What you give up versus £700+ models is coverage, navigation sophistication and scheduling — not basic dependability.

When do robot mowers go on sale in the UK?

Spring launch promotions (March–May), Prime Day (July), and Black Friday (November) are the consistent windows. Navimow was running below-RRP prices on several models when we last checked in July 2026.
