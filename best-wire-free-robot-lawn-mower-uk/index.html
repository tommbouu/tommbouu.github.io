What to check before buying any wire-free mower

  • Sky view: stand on your lawn and look up. Mostly open sky → RTK is fine. Mostly canopy → LiDAR, camera or wired.
  • Coverage headroom: buy a rating at least ~25% above your actual lawn size, or the mower runs flat-out daily.
  • Slope, measured: rise ÷ run × 100. Over ~30%? You need AWD (see the slopes guide).
  • Lawn edges: vision-based budget models (OcuMow) need a defined edge — paving, gravel or a 6cm+ raised border.
  • Outdoor socket: every dock needs power. No outdoor socket is a real, often-forgotten cost — except the OcuMow, which charges indoors.

Common mistakes UK buyers make

  • Buying at 100% of rated capacity — a 500m² mower on a 500m² lawn never rests and loses ground every rainy week.
  • RTK under heavy canopy — the single biggest cause of wire-free regret. Be honest about your trees.
  • Ignoring the i2-series reshuffle — several 2025 guides still recommend the i108E at £1,299 when the i208 AWD does more for £899.
  • No-name imports — no UK support, no spares, no resale value. The OcuMow is the credible floor.
  • Camera mowers on damp, bumpy lawns — reviewers specifically flag the Eufy E15's grip on dewy grass; match the machine to your terrain.

FAQs

Are wire-free robot mowers reliable in the UK?

The current RTK and vision generation is mature: the Segway Navimow i105E alone has 2,000+ user reviews averaging 4.4★ per 2026 roundups. The main reliability factor is your garden — RTK models want reasonable open sky, so heavy tree cover points you towards LiDAR or camera navigation instead.

What's the cheapest wire-free robot mower worth buying in the UK?

The LawnMaster OcuMow 16 at around £292 is the cheapest credible option, suited to simple lawns up to 150m² with a defined edge. Below that price we haven't found anything we'd recommend.

Do wire-free mowers need Wi-Fi?

Most need Wi-Fi or Bluetooth for setup and app control, and RTK models use satellite positioning plus a base-station link. The Worx Vision Cloud is notable for taking RTK correction from the cloud with no garden base station. Check connectivity requirements per model if your garden has poor coverage.

Can I still buy a boundary-wire mower in 2026?

Yes — wired models like the Flymo EasiLife and Gardena Sileno ranges are cheaper and work under heavy tree cover, and Husqvarna's dealer-backed Automowers remain the shade-garden standard. See our boundary wire vs wire-free explainer for when wired still wins.
