Quick verdict

The OcuMow 16 answers a simple question: what's the least you can spend on robot mowing without buying junk? About £292, it turns out. No wire, no app, no outdoor socket — drop it on the lawn, its camera finds the grass, and you carry the battery indoors to charge. Every limitation is honest and printed on the box: 150m², random pattern, needs a defined lawn edge. For a courtyard or terrace-house lawn, that's not a compromise; it's a perfect fit.

How "drop and mow" actually works

Optical Grass Recognition — the camera literally looks for grass and stays on it. The catch is containment: it needs a clearly defined edge (paving, gravel or a 6cm+ raised border) around the full perimeter, because there's no wire, satellite map or virtual boundary to stop it wandering. If your lawn bleeds into flowerbeds at grass level, this is the wrong machine.

Living with it

  • Charging: the MX 24V battery comes indoors to a fast charger — no dock, no outdoor electrics. Budget a battery swap per session on bigger lawns, or buy the dual-battery kit (rated to 300m²).
  • Cutting pattern: random, up to 4 hours a session. On 150m² that's fine; it gets everywhere eventually.
  • No app: genuinely none. For some buyers that's the entire appeal.
  • Edges: like all robot mowers it won't do them — keep the strimmer.

Who it's for

  • Perfect: paved-edged urban lawns under 150m², buyers who want zero tech, second lawns/front gardens.
  • Wrong machine: lawns over 300m², undefined edges, slopes, anyone who wants scheduling — at that point the £699 Navimow i105E is the honest next step.

Alternatives to consider

  • Flymo EasiLife GO 250 (~£424, wired): proper docking and scheduling if you'll tolerate a wire install.
  • Worx Landroid Vision Cloud (£699.99): the step up in wire-free camera mowing — 300m², app, self-docking.

FAQs

Does the LawnMaster OcuMow 16 need a boundary wire?

No wire, no app, no outdoor socket. Its camera and Optical Grass Recognition software identify grass and keep it on the lawn — but it does need a clearly defined edge (paving, gravel or a raised border of at least 6cm) around the full perimeter to know where to stop.

How big a lawn can the OcuMow 16 handle?

Up to 150m² with the single-battery kit, or up to 300m² with the dual-battery version. It mows in a random pattern for up to 4 hours per session.

How does it charge without an outdoor socket?

You bring the MX 24V battery indoors to its fast charger — the same battery platform as other LawnMaster tools. That's a feature (no outdoor electrics needed) and a chore (it doesn't self-dock) in one.

Is a £292 robot mower actually any good?

Within its limits, yes — it's the cheapest robot mower in the UK with a real brand, UK support and spares behind it. It cuts a small, simple, well-edged lawn perfectly happily. It is not a scheduling smart-home device, and it won't suit complex or large gardens.
