Do Robot Lawn Mowers Work in the Rain?

British buyers ask this before anything else, sensibly. Short answer: rain won't hurt a robot mower, but most are better off not mowing in it — and a couple of models specifically dislike damp lawns.

How mowers handle rain

  • Rain sensors: many models detect rain and return to dock, resuming when things dry out. Behaviour varies by model — the Eufy E15, for example, officially auto-recalls in rain and low light — so check the spec sheet for the exact model you're buying.
  • Weatherproof bodies: living outside in drizzle is what these machines are designed for.
  • Covered docks: some (e.g. the Eufy E15's covered base station) shelter the mower between runs — a genuinely useful UK feature.

Why mowing wet grass is a bad idea anyway

Wet grass tears instead of cutting cleanly, clippings clump and smear, and wheels — robot or human-pushed — lose grip. On slopes this becomes a safety and stuck-mower issue. The practical UK pattern: let the mower skip rainy windows; because robots cut daily-ish, missing a day costs nothing.

The rain-ready setup

  1. Enable rain delay if the model offers it.
  2. Schedule generously (daily windows) so skipped days self-heal.
  3. Consider a dock canopy; position the dock out of the splash zone.
  4. Steep + wet = the case for AWD — see the slopes guide.

FAQs

Will rain damage a robot lawn mower?

No — robot mowers are built to live outdoors and are weatherproof to their rated standard. Rain doesn't harm them; the question is whether mowing in rain produces a good cut (usually not) and whether wheels grip wet grass.

Should I let my robot mower cut wet grass?

Generally no: wet clippings clump, cuts tear rather than slice, and traction drops — especially on slopes. Most owners set rain-delay so the mower waits for the lawn to drain.

Can robot mowers stay outside all winter in the UK?

Manufacturers generally recommend indoor winter storage for the mower and protection for the dock. Cold storage preserves the battery. See our winter storage checklist for the routine.

Which robot mowers handle wet UK lawns best?

Models with rain sensors plus good tread. The Gardena Sileno line is explicitly marketed as mowing in rain; reviewers specifically flag the Eufy E15's wheel grip on damp grass as a weakness. AWD Mammotion models hold traction best on wet slopes.
