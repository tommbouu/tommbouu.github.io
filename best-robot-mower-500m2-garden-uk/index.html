Value pick: Navimow i108E

At its £549 promo price (£649 list) with a coverage rating well above 500m² rating verification pending, the i108E is the rare case where the bigger mower is also the cheaper one. Same RTK + vision platform as the rest of the i-series.

The Eufy E15 case

800m² rating, no-antenna 15-minute setup, superb obstacle avoidance. The caveats from our E15 comparison stand: flat, even lawns only — at 500m² your lawn probably has more character than the E15's wheels enjoy, so be honest about your terrain.

Why wire-free wins at this size

A 500m² lawn implies ~90–120m of perimeter plus islands. Wired, that's a weekend of pinning wire (or an installer's fee — see installation costs). Wire-free, it's under an hour with your phone. The bigger the lawn, the bigger the wire tax.

What to avoid

  • Any mower rated exactly 500m² unless your lawn is a simple open rectangle.
  • Ignoring zones: if your 500m² splits front/back, confirm multi-zone support and transfer routes.

FAQs

Can the Navimow i105E handle a 500m² lawn?

It's rated exactly 500m², so technically yes — but it would mow at full stretch every day with no slack for weather, growth spurts or complex shapes. At 500m² we recommend stepping up to a higher-rated model like the i108E.

What's the cheapest robot mower for 500m²?

With proper headroom, the Navimow i108E at £549 promo (~800m² rated, verify) is the value leader as of July 2026. Wired, the Gardena Sileno Life 750 covers 750m² — price pending our verification.

Should a 500m² garden go wired or wire-free?

At this size wire-free earns its premium: 500m² means roughly 90–120m of perimeter wire to bury, plus islands around beds and trees — a full weekend or an installer's invoice. The app walk-round takes under an hour.
