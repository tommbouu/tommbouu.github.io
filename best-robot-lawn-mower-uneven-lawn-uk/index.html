What actually matters on rough ground

  • Wheel size and tread: bigger, knobblier wheels bridge hollows instead of falling into them.
  • Ground clearance: low decks scalp humps and ground the chassis.
  • Drive: AWD keeps traction when two wheels lift on a hump.
  • Cutting height range: start high on uneven lawns and come down over weeks.

The model to think twice about

The Eufy E15 is a genuinely good mower on flat lawns — but multiple professional reviews (TechRadar, Tom's Guide) report it struggling with "only lightly uneven terrain" and losing wheel grip on damp grass. On a bumpy lawn, that's a daily rescue mission. Buy it for flat gardens; skip it here.

Ten-minute lawn prep that helps any mower

  1. Fill mower-trap holes (a bucket of topsoil, tread it in).
  2. Knock down sharp ridges — they're what actually beaches a mower.
  3. Do the first cuts at maximum height, lowering weekly.

FAQs

Can robot mowers handle bumpy lawns?

Yes, if you pick for it: larger wheels, higher ground clearance and ideally AWD. Flat-lawn camera models are the wrong choice — reviewers note some struggle on even lightly uneven terrain.

Will a robot mower scalp high spots on my lawn?

It can if the cutting deck is low over a bump. Raise the cutting height initially, and consider topdressing the worst humps — robot mowers actually encourage a leveller lawn over time because frequent light cuts even out growth.

Should I level my lawn before buying a robot mower?

Fix only the extremes: holes that could beach the mower and sharp ridges. You don't need a bowling green — mowers built for uneven ground handle normal British lumpiness fine.
