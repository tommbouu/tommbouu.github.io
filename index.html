Boundary wire vs wire-free — the 60-second version

Wired mowers (Flymo, Gardena, most Husqvarnas) follow a wire you bury around the lawn's edge. Cheaper machines, proven tech — but the install takes a day (or an installer's fee), and a broken wire means hunting the break with a radio.

Wire-free mowers (Navimow, Mammotion, Eufy, Worx Vision) navigate by RTK GPS, LiDAR or cameras. Setup is an app walk-around measured in minutes, and changing your lawn layout is a remap, not a re-dig. The trade-off: RTK models want reasonable sky view, and prices start higher.
