First: measure your slope

Manufacturers rate slopes in per cent: rise ÷ run × 100. A 1m plank with one end 35cm higher = 35% ≈ 19°. Quick reference:

What to avoid on slopes

  • Buying at the rated limit — a mower rated 35% on a 35% slope will struggle when wet. Aim to use no more than ~75% of the rating.
  • Camera-only models on banks — the Eufy E15's reviewers note grip problems on merely damp flat grass; it's explicitly flat-to-moderate terrain only.
  • Slick wheels — if the product photos show smooth wheels, keep scrolling.

FAQs

What slope can a robot lawn mower handle?

Standard two-wheel-drive mowers typically manage gentle slopes; AWD models claim much more — Mammotion advertises up to 80% (~38°) for the Luba Mini AWD (manufacturer claim, verify for your model). Always keep a safety margin below the rated maximum, especially on wet grass.

How do I measure my garden's slope?

Slope % = rise ÷ run × 100. Lay a 1m plank downhill with a spirit-level app: if one end sits 20cm higher, that's a 20% slope (~11°). Measure the steepest section — that's the number that matters.

Do robot mowers slip on wet slopes?

They can — wet grass reduces traction significantly, and reviewers specifically flag some camera-navigation models for slipping when damp. On a serious slope, AWD with off-road tread is the safe specification.
