What to avoid on slopes

  • Buying at the rated limit — a mower rated 35% on a 35% slope will struggle when wet. Aim to use no more than ~75% of any published rating.
  • Camera-only models on banks — the Eufy E15's reviewers note grip problems on merely damp flat grass; it's explicitly flat-to-moderate terrain only.
  • Trusting an unpublished slope claim — if a UK listing doesn't state a percentage, don't assume one. Ask the manufacturer directly before buying for a real slope.

FAQs

What slope can a robot lawn mower handle?

It depends on the model and whether it's 2WD or AWD. AWD gives real traction on wet or bumpy slopes, but not every AWD manufacturer publishes an exact climbing percentage — check the specific model's UK spec sheet rather than assuming.

How do I measure my garden's slope?

Slope % = rise ÷ run × 100. Lay a 1m plank downhill with a spirit-level app: if one end sits 20cm higher, that's a 20% slope (~11°). Measure the steepest section — that's the number that matters.

Do robot mowers slip on wet slopes?

They can — wet grass reduces traction significantly, and reviewers specifically flag some camera-navigation models for slipping when damp. On a serious slope, AWD with off-road tread is the safer specification, even without a headline climbing percentage.
